How to Get Started in Hacking

October, 2023
Image

One of the most common questions I get, and one of the most common questions I see online in regarding to hacking is "How do I get started?" There seems to be this invisible barrier between those that know how to hack, and those that don't. The question, is how do you cross that barrier?

I'm here to tell you that there is no barrier. Anyone can learn how to hack, you just need to be willing to put the time in. There is no secret, you don't need to have years of dev experience. You don't even need to know how to code! I have found my own success in the world of Bug Bounty, and I can only write simple python scripts. So, don't be afraid of venturing into the world of hacking and bug bounty. There are people out there that will tell you how hard it is, and will discourage you from trying, but I promise that it is not as hard as many make it out to be.

Anyways, without further ado, here are my tips for getting your foot in the door!

It's going to be hard to start hacking if you don't know anything about vulnerabilities, so familiarize yourselve with OWASP Top Ten. These are the most common vulnerability types, and are a great starting place. However, I find the descriptions from OWASP to be very high level, so I would recommend using Portswigger. Portswigger has an amazingly large amount of information and free labs on many vulnerability types, so keep them in mind as a great resource! Here are (in my opinion) the most useful and interesting parts of OWASP Top 10:

  1. Broken Access Control
  2. Cryptographic Failures
